OLED burn-in, in one paragraph

On OLED panels each pixel makes its own light, and a pixel that shows the same bright thing for hours slowly dims relative to its neighbours — a faint ghost of your old layout, permanently. Wallpapers are the classic culprit: they sit unchanged all day behind your windows.

Zenwall leans on three habits:

  • Dark canvas. Black gaps draw no power and can't burn.
  • Movement. Every re-roll reshuffles the whole mosaic, so no image keeps the same spot.
  • Rotation. The pack writes a folder of shuffled variants — point your OS slideshow at it and the desktop is never static.
